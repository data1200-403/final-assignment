Accessibility Report

Introduction

During development, we decided on a simple layout. We developed with mobile first mentality, and made a single column layout with text followed by image. On tablet sized screens (650-1080px) we made images smaller and increased the deadspace on the borders to increase readability without excesssive scrolling.

On larger screens we considered the possibility of users having ultra-wide displays and limited the width of the content to 960px, preventing content from eating excessive space on large screens. Otherwise this is a two column layout.

Accessibility auto-check

We used Wave by WebAIM and AChecker to assess our primary four pages (not Gallery as this is our equivalent to the "fun" page) to the WCAG 2.0 AAA standard.

All our pages passed this test with only one known problem. The navbar has a duplicate link to the homepage. This is a compromise with UD in mind, as people find different things intuitive. We have also found this type of duplicate (logo/dedicated entry) is normal amongst bigger websites. For any other potential problems/warnings, we went over them and concluded that items such as missing sitemap and changing text directions were not relevant to our site.

Human review

We checked the colour contrast of our red heading backgrounds and found that the original colour #C5192D did not meet the AAA standard, so we changed to #B01527 which fulfills AAA requirement.

Our page has only one distinct colour and that is the red used in the banners and hovered links. We have done a manual colour check with various settings for color vision deficiency and it appears contrast is fine and should cause no conflict.

Our choice of text size meets the AAA standard, and with the limit of 70 characters per line. There are no issues scaling the size of the page to even 300% of the original size. No content or formatting is lost thanks to the responsive design that swaps to the smaller tablet and mobile versions.

Screen readers

We did manual screen reader testing with both native Windows and Mac narrators. While challenging to learn, we found the narrators followed the natural order of content. Notably we also have the "current page" spoken in the relevant navigation link as well as a toggleable mobile menu through the screen reader.

We tried to navigate around the pages using keyboard only, and were not able to trap ourselves, or fail to access any part of the pages including opening all links in new tabs.

Specific decisions

We replaced a Youtube embed, after verifying the automatic CCs being acceptable, with an image due to page loading concerns on mobile devices through PageSpeed Insights. We also changed all our images to WEBP format to adhere to modern standards and reduce bandwidth usage.

Conclusion

We set out with the goal to achieve WCAG 2.1 level AA accessibility, and fulfill as many AAA criteria as practicable, and we feel we have achieved this goal.

While the AChecker checks were WCAG 2.0, we manually reviewed the revisions for version 2.1.
